How to Play

Map

You will start the game on the map. You will work your way to the top of the map by completing different encounters. You must choose a path by clicking on the icons that are glowing red and following that path up to the boss.

Icons

Normal Encounter

The most standard encounter in the game. You will fight three enemies and recieve a card to add to your deck at the end.

Elite Enemy Encounter

A more challenging fight against a single strong enemy. You will recieve a card as well as a powerful relic that will help you become stronger.

Boss Encounter

The final boss of the area. Once you defeat the boss you will gain a powerful upgrade and move to the next zone.

Gold Rush

A more powerful group of enemies await you, but you will be rewarded with more currency than a normal fight.

Treasure

A powerful upgrade awaits you in the middle of each stage.

Mystery Zone

Something will happen to you here that will alter your path forward. It's a mystery so you'll have to try it to find out!

Merchant

A merchant is here to offer you a selection of cards and relics to purchase.

Blacksmith

The blacksmith can upgrade your cards to be more powerful for a price.

Cards

You will start with 12 cards to defeat enemies. After each fight you will be rewarded with a choice of four cards to add to your deck. There are six card types that each have their own unique playstyle.

Offense Cards

Fire

Most fire cards inflict Burn which deals damage over time at the end of each turn. Burn gets reduced by 1 each turn. Fire cards focus on killing enemies slowly over time by adding burn until they die

Lightning

Lightning cards deal a ton of direct damage immediately to an enemy. Some lightning cards make you gain Energize which grants you more mana the following turn. Lightning cards are all about gaining a lot of mana to then turn into damage to unleash upon your enemies.

Negation Cards

Ice

Ice cards focus on inflicting Frostbite which reduces buffs gained by 50%. There are also ice cards that can help you gain a ton of block which helps negate damage. Ice cards are best against enemies that try to gain a lot of buffs rather than outright attcking each turn.

Air

Air cards focus on inflicting Windswept which reduces attack and burn damage by 50%. These cards mainly focus around drawing cards and the Winds of Change card which grows in damage each time it's played. Air cards are best against enemies that try to damage you each turn.

Support Cards

Water

Water cards focus on healing. You can gain Blood Siphon which heals you for 20% of the damage you deal and Regeneration which heals you at the end of each turn.

Earth

Earth cards focus on gaining block and thorns. Block prevents damage by shielding your health. Thorns deals damage back to attackers when they directly damage you with an attack.
